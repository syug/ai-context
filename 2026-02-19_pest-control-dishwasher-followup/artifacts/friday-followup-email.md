**Current situation — 15 days after treatment:**

The treatment has been effective overall. Adult cockroaches have not been seen since 11 February, and the frequency of juvenile sightings has dropped significantly. However, juveniles are still appearing — most recently on 25 February — which indicates that while the treatment is working, it has not fully resolved the problem.

One concern I want to flag is the breeding cycle. Professional pest control gel and powder do not penetrate cockroach egg cases (oothecae) that were already deposited before the treatment. German cockroach eggs typically hatch within approximately 28 days, which means any eggs laid in late January or early February could hatch in early-to-mid March. A follow-up treatment targeting the dishwasher area is standard practice for built-in appliance infestations, to catch the next generation before they can establish.
