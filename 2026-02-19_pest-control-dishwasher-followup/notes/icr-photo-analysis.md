## Page 45: Dishwasher (5 photos) — CRITICAL SECTION

### Photo 1 (Top-left): Dishwasher Interior — Upper Rack Area
Shows the interior of the dishwasher with the upper rack visible. Dark residue/buildup is visible on the interior walls and around spray arm mountings. The interior surfaces show discolouration and deposits that go beyond normal use — this suggests the dishwasher has not been properly cleaned or maintained for an extended period. **Dark particulate matter** is visible on interior surfaces, consistent with the "black particles" the tenant discovered when running the dishwasher on 2/4.

### Photo 2 (Top-right): Dishwasher Door Gasket
Shows the door gasket/seal of the dishwasher. **The gasket is visibly warped, deteriorated, and discoloured.** It does not sit flush against the frame. There are visible gaps between the gasket and the dishwasher frame. This degradation:
- Allows moisture to escape, creating humid conditions that attract cockroaches
- Creates gaps and crevices that serve as cockroach harbourage points
- Indicates long-term neglect and deterioration (gaskets don't warp in days)
- Matches the ICR text comment: "Dishwasher door catch broken, gasket warped"

### Photo 3 (Middle-left): Dishwasher Detergent Compartment
Shows the detergent/rinse-aid dispensing compartment. **The compartment is visibly degraded** — discoloured, stained, with buildup around the mechanism. The door/lid of the compartment appears damaged or deteriorated. This matches the ICR comment: "detergent compartment degraded." Residue buildup in this area provides organic material that can sustain pest populations.

### Photo 4 (Middle-right): Dishwasher Lower Interior / Filter Area
Shows the bottom of the dishwasher interior, likely around the filter/drain area. **Significant dark residue and buildup** visible on the bottom surface and around the filter. Dark particles/debris are present — these are consistent with the cockroach droppings the tenant later observed. The accumulation pattern suggests material has been sitting in this area for an extended period, not freshly deposited.

### Photo 5 (Bottom-left): Dishwasher Door / Top Frame Area
Shows the top portion of the dishwasher where the door meets the benchtop frame. **THIS IS CRITICAL:** The photo shows the gap/interface between the top of the dishwasher unit and the benchtop/cabinetry above it. **There is a visible gap where trim or a frame piece should be.** The area appears to lack proper sealing or a trim strip that would close the gap between the dishwasher top and the countertop. This is the exact location where the missing frame/trim piece was later discovered on 2/20 as a cockroach entry point.

**Key observations about this gap:**
- The trim/frame that should cover the junction between dishwasher and benchtop is missing or incomplete
- This gap provides a direct pathway from the space behind/above the dishwasher (typically unfinished cabinet cavity) into the kitchen
- Such cavities connect to wall voids, plumbing penetrations, and the building structure — all cockroach highway routes
- The condition is clearly pre-existing and structural, not something caused by the tenant in 7 days of occupancy

### Summary for Page 45 (Dishwasher)

The 5 dishwasher photos provide strong evidence that:

1. **The dishwasher was in a state of severe neglect** at move-in — warped gasket, degraded detergent compartment, dark buildup/residue throughout
2. **Dark particulate matter (cockroach droppings)** was already present inside the dishwasher before the tenant used it
3. **The missing frame/trim at the top of the dishwasher** is visible in the ICR photos, confirming it is a pre-existing condition
4. **The structural gaps** created by the warped gasket and missing trim provide cockroach entry/harbourage points that are the landlord's responsibility to repair
5. The dishwasher condition could not have deteriorated to this extent during the 7 days between move-in (2/3) and ICR submission (2/10) — this is months or years of neglect

## Conclusion

The ICR photos provide compelling photographic evidence that the cockroach problem at the property was **pre-existing at the time of move-in** (2/3/2026). The dishwasher condition — with warped gasket, missing frame/trim, degraded components, and dark residue — represents a long-standing maintenance failure. The widespread insect evidence across multiple kitchen drawers and cabinets indicates an established pest population, not a new infestation. The missing dishwasher frame/trim piece, visible in the ICR photos and later confirmed on 2/20 as a cockroach entry point, is a structural deficiency that is squarely the landlord's responsibility to repair.
