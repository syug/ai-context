# Brand Innovation Lab - Technology Experience (TEX) FY26 OP1

## MISSION

The Technology Experience (TEX) team raises the innovation bar for BIL's brands by integrating existing and emerging technologies within Amazon touch points. They partner with BIL pods and work independently to drive brand-funded experimentation, efficiently incubate new products or features, and shape design and innovation-focused roadmaps, all aimed at expanding the Amazon Ads canvas through the creation of customer-obsessed ad campaigns. The team is composed of Design and Technology Managers, Product Managers, Design Technologists (DT), Web Development Engineers (WDE), Interactive Experience Designers (IXD), and UX Visuals Designers (UXD). This unique composition allows for the rapid creation of customer-obsessed prototypes through close collaboration between design and development teams -- from pre-sales to launch phases of campaigns and proactive initiatives.

## SUMMARY

In 2024, TEX delivered global impact across three strategic pillars: campaign innovation, product incubation, and design excellence. The team demonstrated the value of consolidated operations by supporting $180M in revenue across 227 global campaigns, including $16.6M from 9 Lighthouse activations. Our innovation initiatives matured, with Imagainator reaching 60K monthly users and gaining AWS product roadmap inclusion, while our Amazon Live managed-service option enabled international expansion for the Amazon Shopping Video org.

To strengthen our product focus, TEX expanded its capabilities by introducing UX Visual Designer and Product Manager roles. These additions support our expansion to product development, exemplified by partnerships with Amazon Live, CreativeX, and SIP's Product Selector initiative.

For 2026, TEX will transform campaign development by empowering designers to craft bar-raising experiences with no-code tools, creating scalable operations that enable DTs to explore new canvas touchpoints and deliver premium Lighthouse experiences. We'll move to strengthen BIL's technical foundation through proposed BIL-E SDE resources for API integration and pipeline management. TEX's design excellence pillar evolves to the TEX Design program and team, leading research, UI/UX design, design prototypes, and bar-raising design systems to improve overall design operations.

This document focuses on TEX's operations prior to recent organizational changes regarding SIP (Scaled Innovation Program). SIP's Operational details will be detailed in a separate document in Q2'25.

## 1. GIVING TOP BRANDS ACCESS TO DIFFERENTIATED, PREMIUM EXPERIENCES AND EXPERIMENTATION

### 1.1 Expanding premium experiences through product innovation

Amazon is actively developing multiple second screen experiences and technologies. Different product teams have taken on the initiative to connect with Prime Video viewers and meet them where they're at. TEX has identified four key product teams to executing contextual Second Screen campaigns: IVA, Xray, Prime, and Shop the Show (STS).

IVA enables viewers to interact with brands using their remote or QR codes, allowing seamless cart additions without disrupting viewing. While Xray provides supplementary content for Prime Video viewers, STS offers a more integrated approach by combining AI-driven product matching with viewing content, though it currently faces challenges in brand personalization and user discovery. Prime Video is developing contextIQ, an advancement in Amazon's advertising capabilities, analyzing scene-level context and user insights to optimize ad placement and content delivery. This AI service is initially focusing on improving contextual ad data, with plans to become an internal open-source tool that could enhance cross-team collaboration.

Challenges remain in streamlining integration processes, improving user experience flows, addressing brand personalization limitations, and enhancing the discovery and accessibility of interactive features. The successful integration and evolution of these technologies is crucial to successful BIL campaigns with second screen experiences. To address these challenges, we will take a hybrid approach between innovating in house to best meet the needs of brands and customers alongside influencing product teams to develop the required features. While second-screen is one part of the BIL journey we also believe that we need to work with product teams working on other initiatives to further expand the BIL Canvas.

In FY26, TEX will pursue expanding its partnership with PV and ContextIQ to enable API access for BIL campaigns by writing a PRFAQ to explore the potential this data could have on BIL campaigns. Alongside it, identify within the Amazon canvas 2 top priority products we believe could drive the most benefit for BIL campaigns.

> **2026 Goal:** Deliver ContextIQ integration PRFAQ by Q2'26, establish 2 new product team partnerships by EOY'26.

### 1.2 Accelerating innovation for brands through cross-Amazon Hackathons

BIL Hackathons have proven to be powerful drivers of premium advertising experiences, with 26 ideas contributing to 32 advertiser campaigns and $26.72M in revenue (Appendix A). TEX's success with Imagainator, now reaching 60K Amazonians monthly and selected for Amazon's AI product ecosystem, demonstrates how hackathons can scale innovative concepts.

For 2026, we'll expand hackathons through strategic partnerships with APM and Prime Video, focusing on premium advertising opportunities like Sponsored PDP and Second Screen innovations. This cross-Amazon approach ensures our prototypes consider real-world constraints while creating unique opportunities for strategic brands.

Furthermore, we'll formalize hackathon participation, requiring a minimum of two members per pod and 20 hours of protected time per participant. We'll host a minimum of three hackathons annually, with at least two involving cross-Amazon teams. A new BIL Hackathon Playbook will guide execution and partner relationships, while our Hackathon Idea Pipeline will integrate with BIL's innovation framework to track and scale promising innovations.

> **2026 Goal:** Launch a Hackathon Idea Pipeline mechanism, including an official BIL Hackathon Playbook, with 75% of winning ideas progressing through prototype phase by EOY'26. Execute minimum 3 hackathons annually with formalized pod participation requirements.

### 1.3 Creating differentiated brand experiences in emerging markets

TEX's international expansion addresses critical market opportunities and operational sustainability: Latam represents immediate revenue potential, with Mexico facing $5.5M in lost opportunities and Brazil projecting $8.3M first-year revenue (Appendix B). Two Toronto-based DTs will support both markets, leveraging compatible time zones while ensuring Portuguese-language capabilities for Brazil's needs. The DTs will be responsible for scaffolding the locale's technical landscape to efficiently launch solutions while delivering bespoke Lighthouse experiences.

EU Endemic operations require immediate reinforcement after dropping from 6 technical resources in 2024 (4x DTs, 2 WDEs) to just 3 team members today (two L5 DTs, one L6 pending). This reduced capacity cannot sustain Endemic's pipeline of 40 active T0/T1 opportunities ($22.2M). Additionally, EU EntHos's growing non-endemic portfolio requires 3.5 DTs with only 2 currently assigned. Instead of linearly scaling to BIL's needs by hiring additional DTs in EU's already mature Ads market, TEX will empower designers across all locales to provide flexible support where needed. As such, two London-based IXDs will handle 32 standardized campaigns (114 in 2024).

APAC/MENA maintains coverage with four DTs supporting Australia, Japan, and MENA. With EMEA projected to reach $0.65B in AmAds revenue by 2026 and increasing DAA opportunities ($850K pipeline), we'll add one L4 DT to MENA while maintaining L6 oversight for lighthouse campaigns.

> **2026 Ask:** 2x DTs (CA), 2x IXDs or Pod-supplied ADs (EU), 1x L4 DT (MENA)

## 3. CREATING A PATH TO EFFICIENCY OR SCALE

### 3.1 Scaling Design Excellence Through Research and Automation

TEX Design will capitalize on BIL's growing portfolio of always-on experiences with partners like GEICO, Expedia, and Avis. Current success metrics demonstrate the impact of UX optimization -- GEICO's Quote on Amazon program saw a 122% increase in Quote Start Rate (to 1.91%) and 10% higher completion rates. However, with IXDs operating at 106% capacity, UXDs covering 54% of IXD projects, and 8 hours per week spent on non-UXD work, we need a more scalable approach.

For 2026, TEX D will implement an integrated design and research framework that includes standardized testing protocols, design templates, and a UX Insights Repository. This repository will aggregate UX insights generated by BIL and across Amazon's ecosystem, integrating relevant data from teams like Retail, Brand Stores, Prime Video, and Amazon Shopping. This cross-organizational approach enables BIL to leverage proven UX patterns and make data-driven decisions based on Amazon's broader customer experience knowledge. For example, when Retail validates navigation patterns that boost product discovery, BIL can apply these insights to Brand Store experiences.

The repository will be accessible to all BIL stakeholders through a searchable interface, accelerating design decisions while maintaining consistency with established Amazon patterns.

Implementation begins Q2'25 with two strategic pilots: 1.) Travel Deals accelerator and 2.) TEX's "Value of AR in BIL Advertising" initiative. These pilots will validate our research playbook before expanding to 50% of verticals by Q4'25, targeting full implementation and 80% vertical adoption by Q2'26. This systematic approach projects a 15% increase in first-time user success and 5% improvement in Connections' Customer Insights scores. User testing cycles will compress from weeks to days as we expand capacity from 2 to 15 quarterly tests by Q2'26.

> **2026 Ask:** 2x L4 IXD, 1x L5 IXD, 10x UserTesting.com creator seats

> **2026 Goal:** Decrease UXD execution design tasks to 10%, implement phased UX adoption across verticals

### 3.1 Empowering Designers as campaign builders

After evaluating 14 page-building solutions, Webflow emerged as the optimal platform for enabling designers to independently build and launch BIL campaigns without custom code. Starting June 1, TEX will pilot Webflow with US TelEnt, Endemic and EU EntHos, Endemic pods. The pilot will validate three key areas: 1.) Maintaining BIL's creative excellence, 2.) Reducing post-sales development hours by shifting them to design hours with a 45% efficiency gain, and 3.) Establishing clear criteria for when to use the tool.

The average T2-L0 campaign build time will decrease to 75 hours from 110 hours (Appendix C), enabling individual designers to launch 16 campaigns annually -- double the FY24 DT output -- while maintaining capacity for Lighthouse initiatives. BIL's FY25 account plan outlines 409 T2-L0 global campaigns with projected 20% growth to 490 campaigns in FY26. To fully eliminate manual coding from the delivery of these experiences, BIL and TEX require 26 design-focused HC. TEX's current four IXDs, plus three additional hires listed in 3.1, will create capacity for 112 designer-led campaigns in FY26. While TEX's 3 UXDs could deliver an additional 48 campaigns, this would compromise focus on systems development and product improvements critical for DAA commitments. Instead, equipping BIL's 14 CA, IX, AU, JP ADs with no-code expertise will unlock capacity for 192 campaigns yearly.

As BIL's no-code approach matures and demonstrates stability, we'll expand our program to DCS designers and begin transitioning T3 campaigns in Q2'26, reducing BIL creatives' long-term page-building responsibilities.

> **2026 Ask:** 20 global IXDs, ADs, and Visual Designers onboarded to deliver no-code landing pages

> **2026 Goal:** Achieve 55% of T2-L0 campaigns built without custom code by EOY'26

### 3.2 Scaling campaign development operations with Project Providence

Currently, BIL lacks an efficient way to monitor the status, location, or ownership of BIL-owned Brand Store pages, leading to operational inefficiencies. This became evident during 2024's accessibility compliance initiative, where simply identifying pages and components owned by BIL consumed 120 hours of DT/IXD time and deprecation of inactive pages took up to 9 months. With originally 1k+ Brand Stores pages to manually analyze, a comprehensive management solution has become critical. While the long-term vision encompasses all BIL-built pages, Project Providence will initially focus on Webflow-based campaigns.

Launching in Q3'25, Project Providence will serve as BIL's central Campaign Hub for designer-built page management. The hub will track Brand Store URLs, publication status, ownership, localization management, and analytics for Webflow-built pages. Providence's middleware layer will translate these Webflow-exported pages into self-hosted assets for seamless Brand Store integration.

A key strategic decision is the deliberate decoupling of Providence from Webflow-specific functionality. As BIL pilots Webflow for non-DT landing page development, this separation ensures the hub's long-term viability. The isolated middleware layer will allow for seamless transition to potential alternative solutions while preserving core page management capabilities.

In 2026, Project Providence, if successful, will evolve to support a centralized brand style management platform, incorporating brand style guides and design tokens. This foundation will enable two strategic initiatives: 1.) Integration with TEX's forthcoming design system, allowing application of brand tokens across Figma and development environments, and 2.) Enhancement of GenAI tooling, particularly in pitch deck generation, where brand style guides will inform visual styling and tone of voice.

> **2026 Ask:** 1x L6 DT (reallocated from 3P API development) for Project Providence development and maintenance

> **2026 Goal:** Launch Providence with 100% Webflow page coverage by Q1'26, implement brand token system integration by Q2'26

### 3.3 Scaling BIL's Technical Infrastructure for Innovation

As BIL continues to grow, it must delineate DT and Software Development Engineer (SDE) responsibilities to maintain technical excellence and advertiser trust. Two areas require transition to SDE ownership: revised Code Pipeline parameters and API orchestration, particularly 3P.

The current approach to CI/CD pipelines demonstrates this misalignment. TEX DTs maintain 124 production pipelines (Appendix D) for campaigns compared to Brand Stores' 86 pipelines supporting 800,000 stores. Pipeline management, which requires DevOps expertise, adds 5+ hours of operational overhead per campaign. The S-Team "pipeline freshness" goal, requiring pipeline deployments every 16 days, compounds this burden. These infrastructure responsibilities fall outside the Amazon DT's creative technology focus and should transition to SDE ownership. For 2026, TEX and BIL-E will define clear pipeline criteria, limiting their use to always-on products, AWS service deployments, and campaigns exceeding 6-month lifecycles.

API development represents another area requiring SDE expertise. DTs currently own 7 of 9 third-party API integrations tied to $25M in FY24 ad spend (Appendix E), despite the minimal backend engineering expertise defined in their job family. The 8/16 Avis outage, affecting 210 customers, and lack of monitoring on several 3P integrations demonstrates the risk of not having dedicated SDE resources for API development and maintenance. As BIL expands its Non-Endemic advertising offering, reliable API implementations become increasingly critical to maintaining advertiser trust. Dedicated engineering resources for 3P integrations will enable BIL to treat these opportunities as durable product initiatives rather than campaign-specific solutions. This approach balances vertical pods' need for speed with the importance of building sustainable third-party experiences on Amazon.

Similarly, BIL's AI infrastructure requires dedicated SDE resources. The current fragmented backend services, managed by DTs, demand 10 hours per app development and 4 hours of monthly maintenance per service. This architecture, which requires deep AWS expertise, limits standardization and creates InfoSec compliance challenges. In 2026, TEX and BIL-E will develop a centralized AI infrastructure leveraging Amazon Bedrock, reusable CDK constructs, and standardized developer toolkits. This SDE-led initiative will reduce AI feature development time by 50% while ensuring BIL's AI capabilities scale efficiently and securely.

> **2026 Ask:** 2x SDEs focused solely on 3P/1P API integrations

> **2026 Goal:** Implement centralized API management mechanism reducing integration time by 40%, onboard 100% of BIL's 3P API. Standardize GenAI infra reducing maintenance and instantiation overhead by 90% by EOY'26

### 3.3 Scaling TEX's AI Impact: Via Transition and Consolidated Resources

In '24, TEX saw varied success with its GenAI-powered creative ideation tool, Via. Initial adoption in FY24 generated 40 active users per month with 151 total signups. Despite leading pod-specific onboarding sessions and a BAR session to promote adoption, the active userbase shrank to 14 in Q1'25. Our hypothesis is that users have existing preference for tools such as Claude, Cedric, and ChatGPT and do not want to deviate from those options. As such, TEX will pivot Via's focus from ideation to pitch deck automation in 2026. This pivot creates a unique differentiator which can't be supplemented through a chatbot. Via's pilot has provided clear evidence of AI's value in creative workflows; active users report saving 1-6 hours per pitch, with several Via-generated ideas advancing from initial concepts to client pitches. Early experience with Via demonstrates that AI tools are most effective when augmenting rather than replacing creative work, empowering team members to deliver higher-impact creative solutions with greater efficiency.

In Q2'25, the current Via Figma plugin will move to KTLO status, maintaining existing functionality without new feature development. Leveraging Via's technical framework, TEX will launch an AI-powered pitch deck bootstrapping tool by EOFY25. The tool will leverage structured inputs, including BIL's pitch deck template, brand style guides, historical campaign data, and market insights, to automatically populate initial deck drafts with on-brand copy, stylized visuals, and relevant market analysis. This focused approach will accelerate the mechanical aspects of pitch creation while preserving space for strategic creative work, targeting integration with 20% of BIL pitch decks by Q2'26.

> **2026 Goal:** Pivot Via to automated pitch deck creation, delivering bootstrapped decks by EOFY25 and achieving 20% adoption across BIL pitch decks by Q2'26.

## FAQ

### 1. What are your largest paper cuts?

**a. Tech contractor coverage model.** In '24-25, TEX relied on an average of 5 active tech contractors per month to manage load imbalances, at an overhead cost of $1.68M. This approach addressed short-term gaps but came with trade-offs: lower margins, loss of internal knowledge, onboarding delays, and limited strategic continuity.

By 2026, TEX seeks to transition to a more sustainable model: a small group of full-time DT and IXDs not assigned to specific pods but flexibly deployed across regions to rapidly accommodate BIL's most critical campaigns and relieve resourcing bottlenecks. This setup will allow us to dynamically respond to shifting utilization peaks, forecasted up to 145% in certain months, without recurring reliance on contractors. With the rollout of the Webflow pilot, DTs will focus on Lighthouses and complex T0/T1 campaigns, freeing up capacity for long-term strategic work in high-volume regions. Should new bottlenecks arise, we'll respond with scalable levers, such as simplifying upstream designs, reusing vetted solutions, or, when patterns emerge, consider hiring additional resources to address the long-term need.

**b. Third Party Security (TPS) and Application Security Review (ASR) hindering Innovation.** The lifecycle of an advertising campaign is different from that of a product. While a product can afford to go through months of security approvals for a given solution being built, an ad campaign requires a clear idea of feasibility even before it is presented to the client. This is currently not possible due to two processes (Appendix F) that must be addressed for every technical implementation involving backend services, e.g. using AWS, or API integration.

**c. Trading long-term technical innovation for short-term campaign wins.** EU BIL's Loyalty Program was pushed multiple times, along with Localization through AI efforts due to Audi's Showroom, Edison, and Skoda. In US BIL, DTs were required to cancel their Innovation Weeks (2 weeks each quarter) dedicated to exploring new frontiers. Auto DT's OEM-agnostic car configurator experienced a 3-month delay in progress due to work for Nissan Armada and VRBO.

**d. Operational models require refinement to support TEX's growing scope and innovation demands.** While we successfully deliver high-profile campaigns, our current processes create inefficiencies in resource planning and execution. Cross-pod coordination relies on multiple communication channels (Slack, Quip, email) instead of standardized workflows, impacting our ability to quickly shift resources where needed. Technical dependencies and contractor management consume team capacity that could be directed toward strategic initiatives. Most critically, the lack of dedicated innovation funding means new technology exploration must be tied to specific advertising opportunities, limiting our ability to proactively develop solutions that could benefit multiple advertisers. Addressing these structural challenges will unlock greater efficiency and innovation capacity across our global operations.

### 2. What are the hotly debated topics in your area or most difficult judgment calls?

T2-T3 campaign volume vs innovation goals in BIL. A new page builder launching in Q3 will deliver bar-raising landing pages without DTs, reducing post-sales development hours by 50%. This efficiency gain raises a key question: Should TEX accept a higher volume of T2-3 campaigns with this recaptured productivity? BIL stakeholders have yet to align on a clear strategy for low- to mid-complexity campaigns. Meanwhile, self-service SIP products are not offsetting pod workload as expected, leading TEX to anticipate owning continued campaign volume growth. This projection drives the need to double down on productivity innovations rather than accepting increased campaign volume.

### 3. What are your most disruptive ideas?

**Giving advertisers access to No-code tools to create experiences.** Building on learnings from our Webflow pilot and Project Providence, we can enable advertisers to leverage 1,200 certified Webflow partners globally for independent landing page creation. Furthermore, our Brand Tokens system presents a unique opportunity for advertisers to define core branding elements that automatically propagate across full-funnel solutions, ensuring consistent brand experiences at scale.

### 4. What "dogs not barking" worry you?

While timing still is unclear, it's been shared that Asana will be phased out as a project management tool across BIL. TEX, who relies heavily on Asana, will face change management challenges. TEX has developed dozens of processes and workflows around Asana, including integrations for scoping, bandwidth resourcing, and post-launch project capture. Knowing it will not be a perfect 1:1 to Asana, the transition to Microsoft's Planner tool will require extensive effort -- many weeks of time -- to rebuild and retrain partner teams on the mechanisms.

### Appendix F. TPS/ASR process

**a. TPS process** ensures the security of third-party integrations by evaluating data requirements, identifying risks, and guiding teams through InfoSec reviews and mitigations via the TPS portal before final approval. It can take from an average of 6 weeks to a maximum of 5 months. This fluctuation makes any planning difficult. Additionally, we lack a dedicated SM/TPM who can streamline end-to-end ownership of this process.

**b. ASR** ensures the security assessment of 1P applications are respected. A prerequisite of TPS, so this time sums up to the TPS time when a 3P is involved. The current process involves: create a Bindle for resource management, design the software architecture in Design Inspector, classify data in DACE, complete privacy attestation in KALE, submit an ASR application with threat modeling and security survey, work with a reviewer to address requirements, receive ASR upon certification, and for 3P integrations, identify key contacts and submit a TPS assessment with all relevant IDs and information. This process, depending on the data classification and complexity, can take from 2 weeks to 2 months.
