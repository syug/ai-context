# 2025 BIL - Technology Experience OP1

Last Updated: 13-May, 2024

## MISSION

The Technology Experience (TEX) team raises the innovation bar for BIL's brands by integrating existing and emerging technologies within Amazon touch points. They drive brand-funded experimentation, efficiently incubate new products or features, and shape design and innovation-focused roadmaps, all aimed at expanding the Amazon Ads canvas through the creation of customer-obsessed experiences.

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

TEX's FY25 plan outlines priorities, programs, and headcount required to ensure TEX has the capacity to explore forward-looking innovative initiatives, including creative GenAI thought leadership, driving BIL's Lighthouse campaigns, and IXD support for all US BIL verticals. The team is requesting an increase in headcount from 21 FTEs (3 managers, 4 IXDs, 8 NA DTs, and 7 EU DTs) to 26 by hiring 1 US Design Manager, 1 US Design or Technical Program Manager, and 2 US IXDs. Additional IXD HC will support US Non-Endemic verticals. A key objective for '25 is to reduce the need for DT managers to coordinate the team's campaign operations, allowing leads to develop and incubate innovation strategies, particularly at the business vertical level. TEX will continue to build on the success of internal solutions, such as GenAI tools, by dedicating 1.5 US and 1 EU DT to GenAI initiatives by Q2'25.

In Q2'24, the TEX team welcomed BIL's EU and CA DTs. This document has not made HC recommendations to support EU or CA initiatives due to limited visibility into critical business gaps. The team will use the WBR mechanism to identify these gaps and potentially request incremental funding in Q4'24 or fill critical needs through US attrition.

## 2025 PLANS

### Broadening GenAI use cases

TEX recognizes the immense potential of GenAI tools for both operational and creative purposes. However, maintaining a suite of widely adopted GenAI tools requires dedicated resources, not only from a technical but also an operational and program perspective. Currently, TEX maintains four GenAI tools: (1) Via, a pre-sales creative assistant; (2) Foundation, a customizable chatbot assistant; (3) Imagainator, an image generation studio; and (4) AltAI, an image analysis tool to generate accessible alt tags for media. With 40k prompts submitted from 2.9k Amazonians since Q4'23, TEX's GenAI tools indicate strong adoption not only in BIL but also in organizations such as AWS, Devices, Shopping, and Ads.

In '25, TEX will seek to streamline GenAI tools by adding Foundation's unique customization features, including model choice and prompt library, to Cedric, the default Amazon chat assistant. Via will receive dedicated resources to ensure it is creating efficiencies and providing value during the campaign pre-sales and ideation process. Imagainator will extend its image generation capabilities to include audio and video with a long-term vision of providing production asset generation capabilities. Lastly, AltAI, launching Q3'24, will automate the creation of accessible alternate image tags, a '25 Amazon Critical Customer Requirement (CCR), in Figma designs and codebases. To ensure the efficient development of AltAI, the team will coordinate with the 226 Ads members who are also addressing S-Team related accessibility goals.

While internal use of GenAI has progressed smoothly, the team faces headwinds both internally and externally to launch customer-facing GenAI solutions. Today, campaign teams must coordinate approvals with Legal, InfoSec, and PR on a per-campaign basis. InfoSec approvals alone require a minimum of three weeks to evaluate the model's safety. Due to time requirements, TEX will support GenAI inclusion only for BIL's Lighthouse campaigns in FY25. After sufficient data has been collected about launching GenAI campaigns (targeted Q2'25), the team will seek to scale the use of GenAI in campaigns by abstracting up to three tactics, reducing approval and development TOW 50% by EOFY25.

To meet the growing needs of GenAI, TEX will allocate a total of 2.5 DT HC to GenAI efforts by H2'25. Based on execution and pre-sales hours linked to Endemic DTs in Q3'23, devoting 2.5 DT HC to GenAI tools will create up to 105 hours/week for GenAI initiatives. The 88 hours/weekly will be split across four areas: (1) 50% to developing tools, (2) 30% to creating education, developing SOPs, and assisting internal approvals, (3) 10% to MBRs or newsletter-like mechanisms, and (4) 10% coordinating and collaborating with other GenAI teams within CreativeX and Amazon AI (AWS Rekognition / Bedrock). Looking beyond the creative applications of GenAI, dedicated staffing will allow the team to explore how GenAI can assist with campaign scoping, resourcing, and reporting through Amazon Q integrations. Given the immense impact GenAI will have on BIL's business in FY25 and beyond, the current dedicated HC projection is viewed as a conservative investment, focused on initiatives with the highest potential to create efficiencies or raise the creative bar. By EOFY26, TEX leads predict a minimum of 6 HC, including Prompt Engineers, will be required to support the demand of GenAI for BIL's creative and operational needs.

Starting H2'23, TEX will own the creation of a BIL AI MBR. This MBR will serve as a critical tool for communicating updates and insights to Ads leaders, BIL pods, and teams involved in creating both internal and customer-facing GenAI solutions. The document will be created by a rotating set of owners, drawing from BIL's GenAI SMEs and inclusive of all roles, not just DTs. By communicating BIL's GenAI progress to Ads' stakeholders, the TEX team seeks to align visions and raise the bar for Amazon's creative AI tooling.

The average Via brainstorming session lasts 30 minutes, during which the user generates 25 prompts. TEX estimates that 30 minutes of Via usage is equivalent to 3 hours of unassisted design time. With 354 sessions completed in Via during Q1, the tool has generated 1.1k hours in design time equivalency. At its current pace, without accounting for user base growth, the tool will generate the equivalent of 700 days of design (4.2k design hours) in FY24. TEX will double down on initiatives to fuel pre-sales efforts by extending Via to not only create pitch deck artifacts but also assemble slides or decks. The ideation tool will become more aware of individual brands by using advanced Prompt Engineering techniques and Agents (data and context collection bots with internet access). TEX views Via's AI Agents as particularly helpful in the following three areas:

1. **Leveraging Brand History:** For instance, this could enable Via to analyze a brand's past campaigns and digital presence to inform pitch copy and mock headlines.
2. **Content Inspiration:** By scanning a wide range of sources (Cannes award archives, AdAge, etc.), Via agents could provide creative inspiration and reference materials such as images, videos, and articles relevant to the campaign.
3. **Trend Analysis:** A Via agent could analyze current trends in various industries and suggest campaign themes or concepts aligned with currently trending topics. For example, this feature will enable users to input a link to an article about a brand's recent advertising campaign into Via. Via's AI agents can then analyze the content and generate brainstorming ideas on how the brand can further leverage this publicity with Amazon.

Given TEX's GenAI roadmap, complexity, and future potential, the need for a program-focused approach has emerged. This requirement will necessitate roles beyond DTs, such as a program manager, to apply their unique skill sets to Via's long-term roadmap. Program management for BIL's GenAI efforts will also assist in creating systems to quantify the efficiencies gained.

> **2025 Ask:** Reduced US and EU DT campaign capacity to invest in GenAI (1.5x US DT and 1x EU DT HC), and 30% of a Program or Solution Manager's time to assist in roadmapping GenAI.

> **2025 Action:** Drive the creation and dissemination of BIL's AI MBR, abstract 3 customer facing GenAI use cases for repeatability, crisply define GenAI efficiencies gained, and add product rigor to Via's development.

### Fueling BIL's innovation flywheel

TEX aims to proactively inform creative campaign ideation, particularly promoting the use of new and relevant technology in BIL's Lighthouse campaigns. Today, creative teams provide high-level ideas which are then vetted by BIL's DTs. Instead of operating in a reactive state, TEX will codify an innovation flywheel, ensuring that all BIL members are consistently informed about new tactics and ready to integrate them into production campaigns or pitches. To do this, the team will build on their existing Asana-based proactive backlog, feeding output into a communication channel, wiki or internal website, informing teams about recent discoveries. To provide additional input to the team's proactive backlog, TEX will extend its US pod intake system to EU pods and partner teams (e.g., Amazon Live, Prime Video, Beauty Tech, and Softlines VTO teams), broadening the ability for our partners to request concept validation or product incubation.

TEX-led hackathons, starting in FY21, have resulted in multiple achievements for Amazon Ads. Notably, the Kinder Joy AR scavenger hunt and the 'Forward a Reward' concept, which was implemented in the Rewarded Ads product. BIL's Q4'23 Hackathon yielded Imagainator, a GenAI image studio. More than just generating ideas, the hackathons played a key role in fostering relationships across pods, thinking big, and developing future BIL leaders. Compared to BIL's quarterly global hackathon schedule in FY23, TEX will host only one GenAI-focused hackathon in FY24. Two factors jeopardize the future of hackathons in BIL: (1) program management and promotion require a total of +60 hours from the planning members, and (2) pods do not have capacity to devote dozens of hours to completing a hackathon. TEX will continue to promote hackathons as an innovation driver in FY25 by hosting a minimum of two. Hackathons play a critical role in TEX's innovation flywheel strategy, allowing the best submissions to be triaged through the team's proactive backlog for further design and development.

TEX aims to champion the BIL tenet, stating: 'We think long-term when evaluating opportunities, favoring potential earned media impact and future scale over quick wins.' In '25, TEX will drive the recognition of innovation-focused KPIs, such as the quantity of prototypes developed per HC or vertical coupled with their downstream revenue and growth impact across Ads. Revenue targets have been helpful in driving efficiency-focused Brand Store innovations, and TEX is committed to helping BIL pods achieve their revenue goals. However, in '25, OP2 revenue targets will not be prioritized over highly innovative and strategic work, identified collaboratively with pod leads, that provides long-term business impact. Furthermore, the team will prefer exploring prototypes and Lighthouse campaigns across all Amazon touch points, shifting away from a heavy Brand Stores focus and prioritizing expanding the Ads' canvas.

> **2025 Action:** Drive the creation and recognition of innovation-focused KPIs; develop new ways to incorporate hackathons into everyday business; promote innovation across all Amazon touch points and beyond; host a minimum of 2 hackathons in FY25.

### Building on Experience Design momentum

As BIL scales and evolves, the need for UX, design-specific systems, and Design Ops (templates, Figma organization, designer tooling) has become evident. As of 5/10, IXDs are operating at an average of 131% capacity through 10/28. TEX forecasts the demand for IXDs to increase due to three reasons: (1) mandatory Accessibility requirements for all BIL campaigns, (2) greater emphasis on Lighthouse campaigns, and (3) the need to properly prepare design files to remove friction from the production engineering process. As pods work through highly-complex concepts to bring their Lighthouse campaigns to life, IXDs are often required to map out non-linear user journeys, creating the best possible CX when tapping into elements like a customer's camera, GenAI, or a third-party API. The desire for more UX Design emphasis across BIL can be seen in the adoption of the BIL Design Library and anecdotes received about the Interaction Archive. Since its launch in late-Q3'23, the BIL Design Library has seen 16.1k component insertions across all BIL pods, creating 357 days of global design efficiencies. Moreover, TEX's FTV, H1, Brand Store, and Stock Up & Save templates have bootstrapped 660 design projects. TEX's IXDs have supported UX requirements for GEICO's QoA, T-Mobile, Hyundai Evolve/Cosworth, and SIP, demonstrating a need for IXD support across all BIL verticals.

BIL's IXDs will play a critical role in creating paths for Amazon Ads' designers to efficiently assemble accessible page designs. As such, the team will invest in applying Amazon's Critical Customer Requirements (CCRs) to the BIL Design Library and associated templates. Through this effort, IXDs will reduce the additional 25% design TOW encountered by revised Amazon accessibility standards to 10% by EOQ2'25.

TEX will seek to hire one US-based Design Manager and 2 additional IXDs in FY25. TEX's US Design Manager will facilitate the skill and career growth of IXDs, ensure Design Ops excellence, manage accessibility-focused work streams, raise the team's visual design and UX bar, lead programs like the BIL Design Library, and manage resourcing for 6 IXDs across BIL's US business verticals. This plan affords all US pods with 1 ring fenced IXD, creating added bandwidth to support non-US BIL locales when needed. Without the additional headcount, TEX will operate with the current 4x IXD HC supporting US BIL's most complex experiences.

> **2025 Ask:** 2x L4 - L5 IXD, 1x L6 Design Manager HC.

> **2025 Action:** Reduce added accessibility TOW due to Amazon's CCRs from 25% to 10% by EOQ2'25.

### Creating a more efficient campaign design and development process

While TEX and BIL pods have made strides in gaining visibility into opportunity pipelines, allowing TEX to resource more predictably on a 6-8-week horizon, pods do not always ensure that the necessary DT, CAPT WDE/FEE, or IXDs are available to support sold work. Today, BIL pods do not have a mechanism to throttle selling or simplify campaign complexity when builders are operating at capacity. Instead, TEX must reactively augment its staff through contractor hiring. With a 6-week lead time to hire capable contractors, the team must carry the burden of finding resources to build a sold campaign, often for work such as Product Selectors or "cherry on top" repeatable experiences. This hinders BIL's ability to innovate and stalls its innovation flywheel, as all hands are on deck building production campaigns. To help prevent this, in FY25, TEX will steer non-Lighthouse-related work to repeatable or design-led solutions when the team's DTs are operating above 120% capacity. Additionally, TEX-supported campaigns will require DT oversight in the creative ideation and design process, providing technical direction, and ensuring the feasibility and TOW align with the project's objectives.

TEX will not plan for CAPT's Custom Campaign Landing Program (CCLP) to fully own production campaign development in FY25. US and EU BIL pods utilizing CCLP have each experienced similar paper cuts, particularly inconsistent availability, delivery velocity, slow SIM ticket intakes, and inattentiveness to design detail. These paper cuts have led TEX to not fund US WDEs through DT attrition, despite the increasing need for production development. To resolve these paper cuts, TEX will align with CAPT leads on new KPIs which accurately reflect stakeholder satisfaction and program performance, and monitor these KPIs in WBRs. To further improve the program, TEX will continue to invest in the comprehensive WDE training program already established by DTs as a mechanism to uphold DT standards and SOPs. Conversely, TEX will ensure that DTs responsible for developing production code adhere to the engineering standards and best practices defined by CAPT. The standards set by CAPT have added a necessary layer of resilience to DTs writing production code, greatly benefiting TEX. This includes Code Pipelines, ADT-metrics, Code Reviews, maintaining proper commit hygiene, and ensuring that active on-call and resolver groups are associated with each code package.

In Q1'24, TEX supported 73 campaigns across all US BIL verticals. The complexity of resourcing and vetting approximately 5.6 campaigns per week necessitates either hiring additional DT managers or adding a dedicated Program Management role to US TEX. To avoid excessive management layers, TEX will seek to hire a Program Manager to assist with day-to-day operations in the US. The specific job family for this role will be determined while exploring role guidelines for Design Program Manager, Technical Program Manager, and Solution Manager. Ultimately, the selected role will ensure campaigns are assigned appropriately, examine and report capacity, highlight contractor needs, create and maintain intake mechanisms, adjust resources based on shifting campaign timelines, triage work between CAPT and BIL, coordinate semi-annual hackathons, and facilitate Third Party Security (TPS) assessments. Currently, these activities, excluding hackathons and TPS approvals, consume over 40 hours per week of TEX leads' (2x DT managers and 1x IXD) time.

> **2025 Ask:** 1x L4 - L5 US Solutions Manager or Technical/Design Program Manager

> **2025 Action:** Establish unified engineering standards for all production builders, achieve 100% adoption by Q2'25; re-calibrate CCLP KPI.

## FAQS

### 1. What are the hotly debated topics in your area or most difficult judgement calls?

**BIL owning custom campaign necessities, such as Brand Store 1P Pixels and FireTV page builders.** Similar to OPAs, BIL is faced with a useful tactic primarily leveraged in custom campaigns whose future is uncertain. The Devices team is deprecating Landing Page Builder (LPB) support for custom and tailored Fire TV landing pages due to a mandatory hardware migration request. It is unclear whether the team requires funding to migrate LPB's FireTV builder. In the meantime, TEX DTs have validated self-hosted stop gaps while the Devices team finds a new home for the tool. A similar situation has occurred with the deprecation of Amazon's 1P pixels in Brand Stores. With no clear ownership and the need to support Non-Endemic brands like Hyundai, BIL is compelled to take responsibility for the future of 1P pixels in Brand Stores.

### 2. What "dogs not barking" worry you?

**Campaigns risk selling without development and design support.** In slower business cycles, such as Q1, the consolidation of DTs has allowed TEX to arrange support models for capacity overflows, this model will not work during high-volume business cycles. Current ops mechanisms, Asana, Salesforce, capacity dashboards, identify resource constraints after it's too late to secure contingent workers. Furthermore, pods are not ensuring they have TEX design and development bandwidth when pitching or creating work back schedules. In Q2'24, US Autos DTs were required to shift priorities twice to support Booking.com and Avis builds. Notably, US TEX operated at 102% through Q1, a low volume campaign season. In busy quarters, such as late-Q2 through Q4, BIL's pods risk selling work without support to build.

**Allocation of resources for Third Party Security (TPS) approvals.** TEX lacks the capacity to manage the Third Party Security (TPS) approvals needed for launching several BIL Lighthouse campaigns. Facilitating a single 3P approval demands a 100-day commitment from multiple team members, which could be more effectively allocated towards innovation and integration with existing canvas opportunities. Consequently, TEX-led TPS approvals will be reserved for only the most groundbreaking opportunities. With its current staffing model, TEX can support up to two 3P approvals in FY25. To increase TEX's capacity to onboard new, innovative 3P vendors and partners, the team requires a Technical Program Manager or Solutions Manager. Alternatively, 3P approval requests could be handled by a dedicated BIL or CAPT team.

### 3. What were your top misses and learnings? What are you most disappointed about in your area (dirty laundry)?

**Inability to transfer repeatable development and design tasks to partner teams.** Throughout '23 and Q1, TEX has attempted to transfer much of its repeatable work with limited success. The team has looked to offload four templated tactics and widely-adopted tools: (1) Rewarded Ads FireTV experiences, (2) Stock Up & Save, (3) Tina UI engineering, and (4) GEICO Quote on Amazon (QoA) engineering. The receiving teams, either CAPT, Rewarded Ads, or BIL's Scaled Innovation Program (SIP), did not have capacity to support or differing priorities. To further exacerbate friction when transferring repeatable work, BIL's creative teams add varying degrees of customization, invalidating templated solutions. While the Rewarded Ads team has made considerable progress to support BIL's FTV use cases, it does not meet all of BILs requirements and the supporting tool, Rodeo, is on a path to deprecation. Additionally, BIL's US pods aren't aware of the Rewarded Ads FTV templates. Both Tina and GEICO have begun transitions to CAPT engineering, but the process to do so has not been clearly defined by TEX and CAPT leads. TEX views GEICO, maintained by a single DT, as a high-risk priority requiring immediate CAPT engineering support. The model for BIL to own and steer business requirements for CAPT engineering support is not baked and will require additional program management effort to steer back on course.

### 4. What were your positive surprises over the last year? How are you doubling down on them, and what are the specific actions?

**Early success in GenAI for creative presales ideation and pitch generation with Via, BIL's GenAI-based ideation tool.** After a successful pilot in Q4'23, the TEX team launched Via in Q1'24. The tool was quickly embraced by BIL's creative team and various partner teams, including Creative Studios and Amazon Live, to jumpstart brainstorming sessions and create efficiencies in the pitch deck creation process. Due to both the increase in WDE resources for campaign work and the operational efficiencies afforded by the DT consolidation, TEX will be able to double down on Via's impact by devoting DT resources towards growing the tool to a mature product equipped with a strategic roadmap and ongoing development support. By the end of the year, the team plans to refine brainstorming outputs by integrating data from past BIL campaigns and standout industry executions. Additionally, Via will offer tailored brainstorming contexts for specific verticals and brands, and empower users to create custom functions to personalize and streamline their creative workflows. These key developments will be outlined and communicated during TEX's upcoming AI-focused MBR, ensuring stakeholders are kept informed of Via's progress.

**Improved job satisfaction due to combining US DTs.** BIL's US DTs consolidated to the TEX team in November '23. This transition has proven to create a favorable environment for BIL's DTs, primarily due to the shift of technical versus non-technical managers and alignment of technical operations. In Q1, TEX's 9 US DTs completed or made progress towards 35 proactive projects (753 Asana reserved hours), such as GenAI tooling, led pre-sales discovery for 29 campaigns (361 reserved hours) and developed 61 campaigns (3.1k reserved hours). DTs are now spending 1/3 of their time mapped to responsibilities that better align with their role guideline. Additionally, job satisfaction Connections score indicated the TEX team is 11% above benchmark, with 70% of the team submitting favorable responses.

Mid to early career DTs have also shared anecdotes with their managers noting their satisfaction with the new structure.

> *"Since my transition to the US TEX team in late FY23, I have experienced significant improvements in operational efficiency, innovation velocity, and personal job satisfaction -- primarily due to better-defined processes, enhanced collaboration, and technical management who understands the work I do on a daily basis and advocates for me with that in mind."*

While shifting DTs to a centralized model has unique drawbacks, the positive impact on individuals appears to outweigh the negatives after trialing the structure for seven months. As such, BIL leads have chosen to move EU DTs to TEX's consolidated structure. With all US and EU DTs in a consolidated team, TEX will strive to maintain fluid lines of communication with our JP DT peers, ensuring locales share similar mechanisms, knowledge bases, and innovation priorities.

**Prototyping velocity when delivering for partner teams.** The TEX team owns a shared goal between BIL and Amazon Live. The team initially targeted to generate five prototypes to innovate live streaming for advertisers and ultimately scale how BIL incorporates Amazon Live within campaigns. Due to tailwinds generated by combining the US Design Technologists, TEX will deliver up to 10 prototypes by EOY. The ability to rapidly innovate and prototype for partner teams, creating broad benefit for advertisers, will be expanded in FY25. Instead of incubating for a single team, TEX will seek to proactively prototype for three product or service teams.

### 5. What hard choices did you make and decide not to fund?

**Innovative AR/VR initiatives have been deprioritized in favor of GenAI.** During FY22 and early FY23, BIL's DTs made significant progress to unlock unique Augmented Reality (AR) tactics by launching AR scavenger hunts and Virtual Try On experiences. In FY23, TEX had several promising AR initiatives partnered with Amazon Fresh to build a reusable AR game and the Softlines VTO tech team to share a VP-level Kingpin goal, both work streams were halted due to shifting priorities on the partner team's side. With the emergence of GenAI and minimal partner lean in, TEX's time devoted to AR has shifted. GenAI demonstrates a far greater upside for both BIL's staff, advertisers, and customers when compared to AR/VR. However, the team acknowledges that a cohesive innovation strategy must include extended reality goals. This has been demonstrated in Q1 by BIL's US Autos pod's renewed interest in AR. As such, TEX is devoting Autos DT proactive bandwidth to exploring and integrating PlayHybrid's, a 3P AR vendor, 3d models into Brand Stores.

### 7. What are your team's paper cuts?

**Pace of Finance approvals.** In early '24, TEX identified the need for a contractor with a candidate already sourced, Finance approvals and PATH system processing resulted in a 6-week timeline (1/15 - 2/19). Ideally in FY25, with a contractor and business need identified early, this process should take no more than 3 weeks. Prior to this instance, it has taken 12+ weeks to get a contractor onboard, far exceeding the window of contingent workers being necessary. Furthermore, the ability to plan team events, such as dinners and summits, is impeded due to unspecified team budgets. TEX delivered a budget request to Finance partners on 1/17/24 and are still awaiting approval. Until further notice, the team will operate by allocating $3,127/HC for travel and team events.

**Launch deadlines remain immovable despite frequent milestone misses.** The BIL team manages client campaigns that often have strict go-live deadlines tied to product launches or events. Often, in the campaign creation process post-sale, workback schedule milestone deadlines are missed due to clients not submitting assets on time or requesting extra feedback rounds -- setting back all future work by days or weeks. As TEX IXDs and DTs are the final teams to touch the work before launch, they frequently work nights and weekends to absorb these delays and meet these immovable launch deadlines.

### 8. What are your most disruptive ideas (not included in your OP1 plan)?

**Accessibility Bar Raiser tool.** BIL's current standard involves using Lighthouse screenings to evaluate the accessibility of our webpages. However, these screenings often don't fully capture whether the experience is genuinely enjoyable or fully functional for users with diverse accessibility needs. To solve this, TEX can build a tool where the user inputs a URL, and the LLM agent "views" the page through the lens of various personas representing different accessibility challenges. This AI agent would simulate the experience of users with visual impairments, hearing disabilities, motor challenges, and cognitive disorders, among others. After the simulation, it would generate a report detailing the quality of experience from each persona's perspective, helping reviewers identify and rectify areas where the webpage might fall short in terms of accessibility and user experience.

**Using GenAI to create UI layouts in Figma.** Several GenAI UI creation tools have emerged as viable options to create efficiencies in BIL's design process. A particularly interesting tool is Relume. The tool accepts text prompts to generate design artifacts such as UX journeys, site maps, UI style guides, and high-fidelity layouts with direct integration to Figma, BIL's primary design tool. Framer has also released GenAI layout capabilities with the option to generate code. Given these tools leverage OpenAI's APIs, BIL faces a 6-month or longer TPS, business, and finance approval process to onboard the tool. Given the pace of the GenAI application market, TEX will wait for an AWS-backed tool to emerge with the expectation that a viable option will be available by Q3'25.

**Designers owning custom campaign builds.** The TEX team is well equipped to deliver a new page building experience, allowing BIL's designers to launch bespoke and visually appealing landing pages, without production engineering support. Currently, tailored Brand Store and FireTV layout options are limited and do not provide the level of visual customization expected by creative teams and advertisers. A designer-focused custom page builder would feature simple drag and drop components with Ferry Service / Halfpipe component integration. Alternatively, TEX could seek to build Figma plugins allowing the design tool to directly publish Brand Store and FireTV pages. Currently, the CA pod vends the Brand Store Designer tool which the team is using to gather data and inform future page building initiatives.
