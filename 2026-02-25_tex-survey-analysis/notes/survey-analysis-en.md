## 1. Executive Summary

Analysis of the FY26 TEX Discovery Planning Survey responses reveals the following major trends.

**AU Pod (4 respondents: lukthis, Rich, Mark, Chris)** identifies scoping inefficiency, Prime Video integration, and AU market-specific Canvas constraints as primary challenges. A critical structural factor: **Fire TV is unavailable in AU, making 2nd Screen the only viable Canvas for Prime Video integration**. This constraint defines AU Pod's strategic direction -- the focus on 2nd Screen Experience is not a preference but a necessity. Regarding AI, a "selective use" stance is noted, though **this view comes from Rich & Mark (2 individuals interviewed together in a joint session, Creative roles), and may not represent the Pod-wide consensus**. There is strong demand for operations automation (scoping, pitch decks, intake management).

**JP Pod (3 respondents: miyumat (Sr. Art Director), shunakd (Creative Director), hayemiri (Solutions Manager, +1 on miyumat's response))** prioritizes Prime Video Sponsorship (PVS) expansion, cross-platform integration, and AI-powered ideation acceleration. **However, JP Pod's SM perspective is limited to hayemiri's endorsement of miyumat's response; no independent detailed SM input was provided.** This means JP's operational efficiency needs (budget management, timeline management, client negotiation processes) are not sufficiently captured in this survey. Challenges include persuasive proposal frameworks for CPG brands and insufficient understanding of new Ad Menus (e.g., Interactive Video Ads).

**MENA Pod** had zero responses, so region-specific needs cannot be assessed. MENA markets have significantly different cultural and regulatory constraints (Ramadan-linked campaign cycles, content restrictions, Arabic RTL layouts) compared to AU/JP. **Tier decisions made without MENA input are acknowledged as incomplete.**

**Overall, Prime Video integration, AI utilization (operations automation + ideation support), and cross-platform experiences are the three most critical themes for FY26 Discovery Planning.**

## 2. Key Themes

Cross-cutting analysis of all responses identified five common themes.

### Theme 1: Deepening Prime Video Integration
- **AU:** Prime Video is the unanimous top FY26 Priority. Seeking improved 2nd screen experience, floating widgets during TV viewing, and device-TV linkage. **With Fire TV unavailable in AU, 2nd Screen is the only channel for PV integration** -- making this focus a strategic imperative rather than a choice.
- **JP:** PVS Tier 1 acquisition is the top goal. Information bridge between PV, FTV, and Amazon.co.jp is a key challenge. Desire for automatic Sponsored Products recommendations when viewing PV sponsored content. **PVS Tier 1 acquisition pressure is high, with urgency to counter competitors (notably Netflix ad-tier entry).**
- **Handoff Document Link:** Existing campaigns (Maltesers Indecision Duel, Spider-Noir PV X-Ray) serve as live examples of PV integration. These in-progress projects directly connect to Survey-identified Pain Points and can be leveraged as prototype candidates.
- **Common:** Prime Video is the top-priority domain across both Pods, with demand for "experience integration" beyond simple ad placements.

### Theme 2: AI Adoption -- Expectations and Temperature Differences
- **AU:** AI desired for scoping automation, matchmaking, brief generation -- operations efficiency use cases. Some caution around creative/ideation use, though **this comes from Rich & Mark (2 individuals from a joint interview) and may not represent the full AU Pod sentiment**. lukthis (SM) and Chris (Pod Lead) are positively inclined toward AI.
- **JP:** AI actively desired as an ideation supplement. Want AI to surface possibilities that CDs/ADs may overlook. High expectations for 1-pager collaboration idea generation and rapid prototyping. shunakd deeply considers the optimal AI-team relationship, identifying the tension between "team-wide common tools" and "individually optimized workflows."
- **Common:** Both Pods are positive on AI adoption, but AU leans toward "operations" while JP leans toward "creative" applications.

### Theme 3: Scoping/Proposal Process Inefficiency
- **AU:** Feasibility checks for new ideas take too long. Unclear who to ask. Manual pitch deck creation is burdensome. Brief intake information aggregation is cumbersome.
- **JP:** Insufficient understanding of new Ad Menus/technology prevents BIL from full utilization. AI experience implementation takes too long to fit project timelines.
- **Common:** The "idea to proposal" pipeline has significant bottlenecks with substantial room for automation.

### Theme 4: Market-Specific Canvas Constraints
- **AU:** Fire TV, Alexa, Rufus, and LeadGen API are unavailable. Brand Store UX falls short compared to native app experiences. **These constraints define AU's strategic direction, forcing concentration on available Canvases (2nd Screen, Mobile Web).**
- **JP:** New menus like Interactive Video Ads are being introduced, but BIL understanding/adoption lags behind.
- **MENA (estimated):** Arabic RTL layout requirements, content regulations (religious/cultural considerations), Ramadan-linked campaign cycles, and other constraints likely differ significantly from AU/JP.
- **Common:** Canvas constraints vary by market, making globally standardized experience design difficult.

### Theme 5: Revenue Attribution & Measurement
- **AU:** SS (Sponsored Solutions) revenue attribution issues. Discrepancy between manual tracking and automated dashboards. Complex workflow from DSP data retrieval to Salesforce rodeo ID entry.
- **JP:** Difficulty proposing new creative approaches when tied to CPG sales results.
- **Common:** Measurement accuracy and efficiency are challenges for both proposal persuasiveness and operational efficiency.

## 4. Ad Innovation vs Ops Efficiency -- KPI Separation

Per Gemini's review feedback, FY26 TEX Discovery outputs are classified along two axes. **Clearly separating success definitions (Measurement) for each** prevents evaluation confusion.

### 4.1 Ad Innovation (Client-Facing Innovative Experiences)

| Definition | New advertising experiences/prototypes to pitch and sell to clients |
|------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Success Metrics** | Pitch count to BIL, client adoption rate, revenue attribution (ad revenue contribution) |
| **Applicable Candidates** | PV 2nd Screen Experience, Amazon Review-Powered Experience, Maltesers WebSocket, Spider-Noir X-Ray, Cross-Platform Personalization |
| **Kingpin Alignment** | Directly contributes to L10 Kingpin evaluation (CX improvement, client value creation) |

### 4.2 Ops Efficiency (Internal Operations Transformation)

| Definition | Internal tools/automation improving BIL team operational efficiency |
|------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Success Metrics** | Task time reduction %, scoping lead time reduction, tool adoption rate |
| **Applicable Candidates** | AI Scoping & Feasibility Assistant, Title Matcher V2, 1-Pager Generator, Brief Intake Automation, Pitch Deck Auto-Generation |
| **Kingpin Alignment** | Limited direct contribution to L10 Kingpin (however, countable toward TEX Goals "2 prototypes / DT / quarter") |

### 4.3 Strategic Balance

**Recommended ratio: Ad Innovation 60% / Ops Efficiency 40%**

- Ad Innovation is critical for maximizing L10 Kingpin evaluation
- Ops Efficiency delivers immediate impact and boosts overall Pod productivity
- Recommended phasing: Ad Innovation-heavy in Q1-Q2, with Ops Efficiency results accumulating in Q3-Q4
