**Minor note:** The phrase "manufacturing defect" is slightly imprecise. The issue is a packing/fulfillment error (wrong quantities of parts packed), not a defect in the manufactured parts themselves. This distinction is unlikely to matter legally, but "packing error" is more precise and harder for Myer to dispute on a technicality.

## 2. Legal Accuracy

**Rating: Good**

- **ACL Major Failure classification:** Correct. Under s.260 of the ACL, a product has a major failure if it is substantially unfit for its normal purpose and cannot easily be made fit. A bed frame that cannot be assembled due to missing/wrong parts meets this threshold.
- **Consumer's right to choose remedy for major failure:** Correct. Under s.263, the consumer may choose refund, replacement, or compensation.
- **Supplier bears return cost for oversized items:** Correct. Under s.263(4), if goods are too large or heavy to return by ordinary means, the supplier must collect them at their own expense.
- No incorrect legal claims identified.
