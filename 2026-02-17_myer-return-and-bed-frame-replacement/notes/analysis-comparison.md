---

## CRITICAL WARNING: IKEA Mattress Size Mismatch

IKEA "King" beds use European sizing: **180 x 200 cm**. The Australian standard King is **183 x 203 cm**. If you already own an Australian King mattress, it will **not fit properly** in any IKEA King frame -- it will overhang by 3 cm in both directions. This could be a deal-breaker for all IKEA options unless you are willing to accept the overhang or confirm your mattress is IKEA-compatible.

The Koala Brunswick uses **Australian King sizing (183 x 203 cm)**, so your existing mattress will fit correctly.

### Scoring Rationale

**Mattress compatibility:** This is scored as a binary pass/fail with a high weight. The Koala fits your existing Australian King mattress. All IKEA frames are 3 cm smaller in both dimensions. A 4 rather than 0 because the overhang is small and some people live with it, but it is not a proper fit and the mattress may shift or sag at the edges.

**Stairs-friendliness:** Koala and IKEA HEMNES/MALM are comparable -- all have packages around 25-28 kg max and 200-207 cm long. Koala has only 3 boxes (fewer trips) but the widest box is 75 cm. MALM+4 Storage has 7 packages (more trips, more total weight). BRIMNES has a 36 kg package that is a serious problem for stairs.

**Disassembly/moving ease:** HEMNES wins here -- solid pine holds screws through multiple cycles. Koala's tool-free clips are convenient but may loosen after 2-3 cycles. Particleboard IKEA frames degrade with each disassembly.

**One-person assembly:** Koala's tool-free system is a clear winner -- 15-30 minutes, no tools. IKEA frames take 1-3 hours with Allen keys. BRIMNES is 4-10+ hours and reviewers call it confusing.

**Total cost:** MALM basic at $499 + $49 delivery = ~$548. HEMNES at $699 + $49 = ~$748. Koala at $1,090 + $245 premium delivery = ~$1,335 (or $1,090 + free standard delivery but then you carry it yourself). If you add IKEA assembly service (~$120), IKEA costs approach $670-$870 total.

**Return/risk mitigation:** Koala's 120-night trial is excellent -- sleep on it, and return for a full refund if unhappy. IKEA offers 365-day returns but you must disassemble and repack, which is harder. Both are strong, but Koala's trial is more practical for a "try it and see" approach.

## "What Could Go Wrong" Section

### Koala Brunswick

1. **Premium delivery may not cover 3rd floor walk-up.** If they refuse or charge extra, you are carrying 3 large boxes yourself. Box 3 (198 x 75 cm, 18.6 kg) is awkward on stairs.
2. **Boxes may not fit around stairwell corners.** The 207 cm box almost certainly needs to be opened on the ground floor, with contents carried up individually. This means dealing with packaging waste downstairs.
3. **Clip connectors may loosen** after disassembly for your move-out, making the frame wobbly at your next place. One move cycle should be fine; two or more is uncertain.
4. **Limited reviews available.** Only ~30 Trustpilot reviews for Koala generally. No stair-specific experiences found. You are taking more of a leap of faith compared to IKEA's thousands of reviews.
5. **No disassembly guide provided.** Koala markets assembly but does not document disassembly. You will be figuring out the reverse process yourself.
6. **Price premium.** At $1,090, it is more than double the MALM. If you are only staying 12 months and then moving, the cost-per-use is significantly higher.

### IKEA HEMNES

1. **Your mattress will not fit properly.** The HEMNES takes a 180x200 cm mattress. Your Australian King (183x203 cm) will overhang by 1.5 cm on each side and 3 cm at the foot. This may cause the mattress to sit unevenly, slide, or sag at unsupported edges.
2. **The 205 cm package** may be tight in stairwell corners, similar to Koala.
3. **Assembly takes 1-3 hours and requires tools.** If you have never assembled IKEA furniture, it can be frustrating. Not difficult, but tedious.
4. **Slats can slip out of position** (common LUROY complaint). This is annoying but not a structural issue -- can be fixed with anti-slip tape or upgraded slats.
5. **Delivery to room ($49) may not cover 3rd floor walk-up.** IKEA's "into home" delivery is unclear on walk-up apartments. Drivers may refuse.
6. **Solid pine is heavier per piece than expected** if it absorbs moisture. The 27.6 kg package is near the limit for comfortable one-person carry on stairs.

### IKEA MALM (Basic)

1. **Same mattress fit problem** as HEMNES (180x200 vs 183x203).
2. **Particleboard construction will degrade** with disassembly. Cam-lock holes in particleboard strip easily. After one disassembly/reassembly cycle, the frame may squeak or feel less solid.
3. **Known squeaking issues** reported in reviews even without disassembly.
4. **Cheaper feel** compared to HEMNES or Koala. Particleboard with acrylic paint does not age well.
5. **Same stair/delivery uncertainties** as HEMNES.

### IKEA BRIMNES (Any Variant)

1. **Heaviest packages (36+ kg) are a serious stair problem.** The headboard variant has a 38.4 kg package. This requires two strong people on stairs and risks injury or wall damage.
2. **Assembly is notoriously difficult** -- 4-10+ hours, confusing instructions. Multiple reviewers describe it as a "challenge."
3. **Same mattress fit problem** as all IKEA options.
4. **Total weight (113-151 kg)** means many heavy trips up stairs.
5. **Disassembly is complex** due to integrated storage drawer mechanisms.
6. **Not recommended for this situation.**

### IKEA MALM + Storage Variants

1. **More packages = more trips up stairs** (6-7 packages vs 5 for basic MALM).
2. **Higher total weight** (87-104 kg).
3. **Storage drawers add assembly complexity** and more parts that can fail during disassembly.
4. **Overkill for a 12-month rental** -- the storage is nice but adds cost, weight, and moving difficulty.

## Recommendation

### Primary Recommendation: Koala Brunswick ($1,090)

**Why:** Your existing Australian King mattress fits it. This is the single most important factor -- buying an IKEA frame means your mattress does not fit properly, and buying a new IKEA-sized mattress to match would cost $300-$1,000+ on top of the frame price, eliminating any cost advantage.

Additional advantages:
- **Tool-free 15-30 minute assembly** -- critical for doing it alone on a weekday evening
- **Lightest total weight** (59.2 kg product weight)
- **Only 3 boxes** -- fewer trips up stairs
- **120-night trial** -- if it does not work out, return it
- **No single piece heavier than ~20 kg** once unboxed
- **Disassembles without tools** for your move in 12 months

**Before buying, you MUST:**
1. Call Koala and confirm premium delivery covers a 3rd floor walk-up with no elevator, and what it costs
2. Measure your stairwell landing/turn dimensions (if any turn is less than ~110 cm diagonal, plan to unbox on the ground floor)

### If Budget Is the Deciding Factor: IKEA HEMNES ($699)

**Only if** you can accept the 3 cm mattress overhang or are willing to buy an IKEA-sized mattress. The HEMNES is the best IKEA option because:
- Solid pine survives multiple moves (unlike particleboard MALM/BRIMNES)
- Lightest IKEA option (60.6 kg total)
- 10-year warranty
- Well-reviewed and proven design

**Avoid:** BRIMNES (too heavy, too complex), MALM + Storage variants (unnecessary weight/complexity for a 12-month rental).

### If Budget Is Extremely Tight: IKEA MALM Basic ($499)

Cheapest option at $499 but comes with the mattress fit problem and particleboard durability concerns. Acceptable as a disposable frame for 12 months if you plan to sell/discard rather than move it.

## Bottom Line

The mattress compatibility issue makes this decision simpler than it first appears. Since you already have an Australian King mattress, the Koala Brunswick is the only frame that fits it without compromise. It also happens to be the easiest to assemble alone and one of the lightest to carry upstairs. The $1,090 price is higher than IKEA, but buying a new IKEA-compatible mattress to save on the frame would cost more overall and waste the mattress you just received.

If you are open to living with a 3 cm mattress overhang on an IKEA frame, the HEMNES at $699 is the strongest IKEA alternative due to its solid wood construction and proven durability across multiple moves.
