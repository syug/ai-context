## 3. Assembly Process

### Tool-Free Assembly

Koala's bed bases (including the Brunswick) feature a **tool-free assembly system**. This is a major selling point across their range. The mechanism works as follows:

- **No tools required**: No Allen keys, screwdrivers, or bolts.
- **Clip/pin connectors**: Pieces connect via integrated metal clips, pins, or locking mechanisms built into the frame components. You push, click, or slide pieces into place.
- **Assembly time**: Koala advertises assembly in roughly 10-15 minutes, though real-world reviews suggest 15-30 minutes is more realistic for a first attempt.
- **One-person assembly**: Koala markets these as assemblable by one person, though reviews from Trustpilot indicate some customers found it easier with two people, particularly for holding the headboard steady while connecting side rails.

### Typical Assembly Steps (Based on Koala Bed Base Range)

1. Attach legs to the base frame / support beams
2. Connect side rails to the headboard using the clip/pin system
3. Connect side rails to the foot rail / end piece
4. Place the centre support beam(s) across the frame
5. Lay the slat system across the support structure
6. Place mattress on top

### Assembly Quality Notes (from Trustpilot Reviews)

- **Positive**: A 68-year-old customer assembled a Koala bed base with "minor help" - suggests the tool-free system is genuinely accessible.
- **Positive**: Multiple reviewers confirmed "easy assembly" and praised the instructions.
- **Negative**: One reviewer reported "a required screw hole was missing, making it impossible to assemble" - though this appears to be a manufacturing defect rather than a design issue.
- **Positive**: Replacement parts were sent when needed, suggesting Koala's customer service handles assembly issues.

## 4. Disassembly

### Can It Be Reversed?

**Yes, with caveats.** Tool-free clip/pin systems are generally designed to be reversible - you unclip or slide the pieces apart in reverse order. However:

- **Clip fatigue**: Repeated assembly/disassembly (more than 2-3 times) can loosen clip connectors. Metal clips are more durable than plastic ones. Koala typically uses metal connectors.
- **No official disassembly guide**: Koala does not prominently advertise disassembly as a feature. Their messaging focuses on initial assembly ease.
- **Practical reality**: Most bed-in-a-box bed bases with clip/pin systems can be disassembled 2-4 times without issues. Beyond that, connectors may become loose and the frame may develop wobble.
- **Comparison**: Traditional bolt-together bed frames (like IKEA) can be disassembled and reassembled indefinitely because bolts can be retightened. Clip systems are more convenient but less durable for repeated cycles.

### Disassembly for Moving

For a single move (assemble at delivery, disassemble when moving out, reassemble at new place), the Brunswick should handle this without problems. The concern would be if you expect to move frequently (e.g., every 6-12 months).

## 5. Carrying Up 3 Flights of Stairs - The Critical Assessment

### Box-by-Box Analysis

| Box | Weight | Length | Width | One Person? | Two People? | Stair Difficulty |
|-----|--------|--------|-------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Box 1 | 26.9 kg | 207 cm | 26 cm | Difficult - very long | Yes, manageable | **HIGH** - 207cm is the concern for stairwell corners |
| Box 2 | 24.3 kg | 187 cm | 50 cm | Difficult - wide and heavy | Yes, manageable | Moderate - shorter than Box 1 but wider |
| Box 3 | 18.6 kg | 198 cm | 75 cm | Very difficult - wide and long | Yes, manageable | **HIGH** - 75cm wide AND 198cm long is unwieldy |

### The 207cm Problem (Box 1)

This is the biggest concern for a walk-up apartment:

- **Typical stairwell width in Surry Hills apartments**: 90-120 cm
- **Typical landing/turn width**: 100-150 cm
- **207 cm box length**: This box will NOT fit around a tight 90-degree stairwell turn if the landing is narrow. You may need to **unbox the side rails on the ground floor** and carry the individual pieces up separately.
- **Once unboxed**: Individual side rails (~200cm long, ~10 cm wide) can be carried vertically/at an angle, which is much more manoeuvrable than the rigid cardboard box.

### The 75cm Wide Problem (Box 3)

- At 198 cm long AND 75 cm wide, this is a large, flat, awkward box.
- It is the lightest box (18.6 kg) but the most unwieldy due to its width.
- Like Box 1, you may need to unbox on the ground floor and carry individual pieces up.

### Realistic Scenario

**Best case**: You unbox everything on the ground floor or in the building's entry area, then carry individual pieces up 3 flights. Individual pieces (rails, headboard, slats, legs) are all manageable for 1-2 people on stairs.

**Worst case**: Trying to carry the sealed boxes up narrow stairwells. Box 1 (207 cm) and Box 3 (198 x 75 cm) may physically not fit around turns.

### Weight Feasibility for One Person

- Each individual piece (once unboxed) is likely under 20 kg
- Multiple trips required (probably 6-10 trips up 3 flights)
- Physically doable for one reasonably fit person, but tiring
- Two people would make it significantly easier and faster

## 9. Key Findings and Recommendations

### Pros for a 3rd Floor Walk-Up

1. **Tool-free assembly**: Can assemble IN the apartment without needing tools. No noise from drilling.
2. **Relatively light total weight**: 59.2 kg across multiple pieces is manageable.
3. **3 separate boxes**: Easier than one massive box. Each box is a separate trip.
4. **Individual pieces are light**: Once unboxed, no single piece should exceed ~20 kg.
5. **Can be disassembled**: For a future move, you can break it down again.

### Cons / Risks for a 3rd Floor Walk-Up

1. **Box 1 is 207 cm long**: May not fit around stairwell corners while in the box. Plan to unbox downstairs.
2. **Box 3 is 198 x 75 cm**: Wide and unwieldy. Also may need to unbox downstairs.
3. **Headboard (~185 cm wide)**: Once unboxed, still a large piece to manoeuvre around stairwell corners. Need to check stairwell dimensions.
4. **Premium delivery uncertainty**: Unknown whether the $245 premium delivery covers 3rd floor walk-up. Must confirm with Koala.
5. **Standard delivery is ground floor only**: If premium delivery doesn't cover stairs, user carries everything themselves.
6. **Disassembly durability unknown**: Clip connectors may loosen after multiple assembly/disassembly cycles.

### Critical Pre-Purchase Actions

1. **Measure the stairwell**: Specifically the width at landing turns. If any turn is less than ~110 cm across the diagonal, the 207cm rail will need to be unboxed and carried separately.
2. **Call Koala about premium delivery**: Ask explicitly: "I'm on the 3rd floor with no elevator. Will your premium delivery team carry the boxes up and assemble? Is there a surcharge?"
3. **Plan for unboxing downstairs**: Be prepared to open boxes in the ground floor entry/lobby area and carry pieces up individually. Keep a box cutter handy at delivery time.
4. **Have a second person available**: While one person can probably manage, a helper makes the stair-carry much easier and faster.
